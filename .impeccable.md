## Design Context

### Users
Researchers, thesis writers, and students who need to check whether cited papers really support their draft claims. They may understand academic writing and citation risk, but should not need machine-learning or evaluation jargon to choose settings.

### Brand Personality
Careful, scholarly, and plain-spoken. The app should feel like a precise reading room instrument that helps users make trustworthy decisions without exposing internal implementation terms first.

### Aesthetic Direction
Refined editorial desktop workspace: warm paper surfaces, restrained typography, and clear evidence-first workflows. Copy should favor user tasks such as "check citations", "find evidence", and "compare checking methods" over terms such as "judge", "ablation", or raw model IDs.

### Design Principles
- Explain every technical choice in terms of the user decision it affects.
- Lead with recommended defaults, then expose advanced details for users who need them.
- Keep model and provider labels consistent across status chips, settings, and result nudges.
- Separate trustworthy academic wording from internal harness vocabulary.
- Preserve offline-first privacy framing while making cloud and local options understandable.
